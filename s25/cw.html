Labs

Each week, you are expected to work through the associated Online Lab. Target dates for completing these are included. There is no deadline for completing these, but the programming assignments, quizzes and coding reviews are based on the information in the lab.

Quizzes

Each week, there will be a paper quiz on the lecture notes, reading, submitted programs, and online lab exercises.

  • Quiz questions will cover the topics from lectures, labs, and homeworks.
  • Quizzes are offered on weekdays, 11:30 am to 5 pm in lab 1001G HN. You must make an appointment to take the quiz.
  • There are no make-up quizzes. Instead, your score on the final exam will replace missing quiz grades (the final exam will also replace a quiz grade when you take the quiz but do better on the final exam).
  • The quizzes are on paper, and you may not use any notes, books, or any computer device (laptop, phone, calculator, smart watch, etc.).

Code Reviews

There will also be weekly walk-throughs ("code reviews") where you explain one of the programs you wrote for homework to a teaching assistant. Code reviews are integral to software design and development: explaining your coding decisions and convincing another it works correctly leads to improvements in the design and lessens unexpected behaviors and errors.

  • Code reviews are offered on weekdays, 11:30 am to 5 pm in lab 1001G HN. You must make an appointment to do your code review.
  • Each week focuses on several programs (see below). You can choose which program you would like to explain.
  • After you choose a program, the teaching assistant will guide you through the code review.
  • If something is unclear or you are stuck, ask questions! Having a two-way conversation about the concepts, the code, and the design are highly encouraged.
  • There are no make-up code reviews. Instead, your score on the final exam will replace missing code review grades (the final exam will also replace the grade when you take it but do better on the final exam).

Code Reviews & Written Quizzes

Quizzes:

Every week, there will be a quiz to be taken in 1001G HN, on the lecture notes, reading, submitted programs, and programming assignments.
  • As the semester progresses, quizzes will include review questions as well as short programming exercises based on the homework.
  • Quizzes are on paper (echoing the style of the final exam and the first pass of many programming job interviews).
  • For the quizzes, you may not use any notes, books, or any computer device (laptop, phone, calculator, smart watch, etc.).
  • There are no make-up quizzes. Instead, the 10 highest quiz scores are used when computing the final grade.

Code Reviews:

Every week, there will be an in-person code review on the recently submitted programs.
  • For each code review, you choose one of the listed programs to explain on the whiteboard.
  • Code reviews are on the whiteboard (echoing the style of many programming job interviews).
  • All code reviews must be taken by the deadline.
  • There are no make-up code reviews. Instead, the 10 highest code review scores are used when computing the final grade.

Final Examination

The final exam is required. It is comprehensive, covering all the material of the course.

  • You must take and pass the final (60 points or more) to pass the course.
  • The final exam will be during the registrar's assigned time slot: TBA in 1001E, Hunter North.

Exam Rules:

During the exam, you should have with you:

  • Your Hunter ID
  • One 8.5"x11" 2-sided page of notes (no origami, no additional pages)
  • Pencils or pens

During the exam:

  • All phones must be face down on the table in front of you.
  • All ear pods and ear phones must be removed.
  • No phones, computers, tablets, calculators, or other electronic devices may be used.
  • All notes and books must be put away in your backpack.

Exam Format:

On the day of the exam, there will be two parts to fill out:

  • Seat Slip: has your name printed on slip. Fill out the remaining information and sign the seat slip.
  • Final Exam: make sure to fill out the front page information.

Both the seat slip and the final exam must be submitted.


The final exam has 10 questions, each worth 10 points. The best way to study is to work through the problems we have done throughout the semester. The questions are very similar to the programming assignments, quizzes, examples from lecture, labs, and the reading.

On the last lecture, Tuesday, 13 May, we will have a mock final exam. It will be similar to the actual final exam, except that the time will be shorter, since lecture is 1 and 15 minutes while the final exam is 2 hours. An answer key will be available later that afternoon on Blackboard.

    How to Prepare

    • Make sure you do all the problems that have been assigned. The problems (or parts of them) make excellent exam questions.
    • Work through the past exams. These (or variations of them) often appear on exams.
    • While you can print your one page of notes, preparing it by hand is an excellent way to study. A great way to prepare the notes is to condense the Skeletal Notes and Focus Questions you have been taking when working on each lab during the semester (these are available at the top of each lab to help you take notes and study during the semester). These are great for reviewing and generating the exam notes. Go through each set of lab notes and notes from each lecture and consolidate your list to fit on a single sheet of paper. Include standard formats or templates for coding constructs and design patterns, as well as samples of recurring themes.
