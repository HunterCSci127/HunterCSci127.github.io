  • Test-outs: If you know the material for this course (see outline and past final exams below), the department offers credit by passing the test-out exam. For more details, including the upcoming test-out dates in January, see the credit by exam on the department website.
  • Weekly Lecture: Tuesdays 10-11:15am, in 118HN, start on January 28.
  • Weekly Lab, Quiz & Code Review: In 1001F/G, self-scheduled via Navigate (no appointments needed first week).
  • Tutoring: Peer-mentoring is available 11:30am-5pm weekdays when classes are in session in 1001F/G. The lab opens for the semester on Thursday, 30 January.
  • This course presents an overview of computer science (CS) with an emphasis on problem-solving and computational thinking through 'coding': computer programming for beginners. Other topics include: organization of hardware, software, and how information is structured on contemporary computing devices.
  • This course is pre-requisite to several introductory core courses in the CS Major, as well as for the CS minor.
  • MATH 12500 or higher is strongly recommended as a co-requisite for intended Majors.
